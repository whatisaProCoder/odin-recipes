Chilli Chicken

Description

Chilli Chicken is a popular Indo-Chinese dish known for its spicy, savory flavors and crispy texture. Boneless chicken pieces are marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, ginger-garlic paste, and spices, then coated in a light batter of cornflour and flour. The marinated chicken is deep-fried until golden and crispy, giving it a satisfying crunch that forms the base of the dish. The chicken is then set aside while preparing a flavorful sauce made from soy sauce, chili sauce, vinegar, and garlic.

In the next step, onions, bell peppers, and green chilies are stir-fried with garlic in hot oil until slightly softened, adding color and crunch to the dish. The fried chicken pieces are tossed into the pan, coated in the spicy sauce, and mixed well with the vegetables. This creates a harmonious balance of flavors and textures, with tender, spicy chicken and crisp veggies. Garnished with chopped green onions, chilli chicken can be served as an appetizer or as a main dish with fried rice or noodles, making it a favorite for those who enjoy bold, spicy flavors.

Ingredients

  • Boneless chicken
  • Soy sauce
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt
  • Oil
  • Onion
  • Red chilli sauce
  • Vinegar

Steps

  1. Marinate Chicken: Mix chicken pieces with soy sauce, ginger-garlic paste, salt, pepper, cornflour, and all-purpose flour. Let it marinate for 15–20 minutes.
  2. Fry Chicken: Heat oil in a pan and deep-fry the marinated chicken until golden and crispy. Set aside.
  3. Prepare Sauce: In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, red chili sauce, green chili sauce (if using), vinegar, and a pinch of sugar.
  4. Stir-Fry Vegetables: In a hot pan or wok, add oil, then sauté garlic, green chilies, onions, and bell peppers until slightly softened.
  5. Combine: Add the fried chicken to the pan, pour in the sauce, and stir everything together on high heat until the chicken is well-coated.
  6. Serve: Garnish with chopped spring onions and serve hot. Enjoy your Chilli Chicken!
