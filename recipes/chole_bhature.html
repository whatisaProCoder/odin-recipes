Chole Bhature

Description

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish that combines spicy chickpea curry (chole) with deep-fried bread (bhature). The chickpeas are cooked in a rich, spiced tomato-based sauce with ingredients like onions, ginger, garlic, and a blend of spices such as cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala. Often simmered until the flavors are deeply infused, the chole is tangy and slightly spicy, with an optional dash of amchur (dry mango powder) or lemon juice for an extra tang. It is typically garnished with fresh coriander, onions, and green chilies, adding both flavor and a touch of color.

The bhature is made from a dough of all-purpose flour, yogurt, and a bit of baking powder, kneaded and left to rest before being rolled out and deep-fried. When fried, these fluffy and golden-brown breads puff up, creating a soft, pillowy texture that pairs perfectly with the chole’s spicy flavors. Chole Bhature is often served with a side of pickles, sliced onions, and sometimes a cooling yogurt-based raita. This hearty, indulgent meal is a favorite for breakfasts, festive occasions, or weekend treats across India.

Ingredients

  • Chickpeas
  • Onion
  • Tomato
  • Ginger
  • Flour
  • Yogurt
  • Salt
  • Oil

Steps

  1. Cook Chickpeas: Soak chickpeas overnight, then cook in a pressure cooker or pot until soft. Drain and set aside.
  2. Make Chole Curry: Sauté onions, ginger, garlic, and green chilies in oil until golden. Add tomatoes and cook until soft, then add spices (cumin, turmeric, coriander powder, red chili powder, garam masala). Add chickpeas, stir, and simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  3. Prepare Bhature Dough: In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, semolina (optional), yogurt, baking powder, salt, and a little sugar. Knead into a soft dough with water, cover, and let it rest for 1–2 hours.
  4. Shape Bhature: Divide the dough into balls, roll each into a small, flat circle.
  5. Fry Bhature: Heat oil in a deep pan and fry each bhature until golden and puffed.
  6. Serve: Serve the hot chole with freshly fried bhature, garnished with coriander and served with sliced onions and pickles on the side. Enjoy!
